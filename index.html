Design Philosophy

Life-centered design
A design principle that goes beyond human-centeredness, and embraces the whole web of life – humans, all organisms, ecosystems, the earth's descendants.

I prioritize lightweight and low-carbon site building to uphold standards of sustainability in a world wide web that has been growing to be a ravenous consumer of energy.

Outside Eye
I'm a newcomer to web design, but I've been absorbing visual design all my life. I have an outsider's eye that sees through convention and sterile templates. I believe novelization depends on the syncreticization of history.

"Before we can discern the new, we must know the old."

Jean Gebser

Cognitive Science Background
That said, I specialized in Embodied Cognition for my cognitive science degree. This is the theoretical paradigm of cognitive science that gave birth to human-computer interaction, human-centered design (see the OGs, Don Norman and David Kirsh from UCSD), and Conceptual Metaphor Theory (see George Lakoff at UC Berkeley).

I did independent research on conceptual metaphor. The whole web consists of metaphor, the desktop, files & folders, even scrolling up and down!

Slow thinking, fast learning
My practice is, in a word, thoughtful. I'm a slow thinker, but I'm deliberate with my intentions and research. When I have a goal in mind, I learn the means (be it programming languages, tools, or whatever) pretty fast.
