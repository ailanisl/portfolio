Overview

Tokyo Fish Market is a local landmark in the Berkeley landscape. Established as a bait shop in the mid-20th century, it grew to a Japanese grocery store with a great selection of freshly caught fish.

image credit: @gunyarakun

Problem Space

The Tokyo Fish Market website retains its old UI that's reminiscent of clunky web design of the early days of the Internet

The Vision

Bringing the online branding presence into the 21st century and integrating an e-commerce function for specialty menu items.

The goal was to maintain its original vintage UI flavor while drawing upon modern Japanese commerce site design.

For the main page, I wanted to create a proxy experience of visiting an open-air Japanese marketplace, or ichiba.

A photo I took of Nijo Ichiba, a fish market in Sapporo!

Research and Design

My research consisted of moodboarding and competitive analysis. Inspiration mainly came from Japanese web design with a playful UI

I also took inspiration from the physical architecture of Tokyo Fish Market. Below is an image of the old storefront of the original building. It had a quaint and antique look that I wanted to bring into the website

In Figma, I recreated the storefront image with vector drawings.

I used Photoshop to get the orientation and skew of the typography just right.

Reflections

This was my first project that I created in Figma, and, while it's not the prettiest or cleanest, I am proud of the unique and easter-egg style that I rendered. I think the final design did a good job of retaining the feel of the original site, while adding a bunch of other features.

I learned a lot from this project and I can only grow from here!
